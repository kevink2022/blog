How I learned better think by thinking less and writing more.

2024-05-04 · Kevin Kelly

“A book, then, or a computer, or a program comes into existence first as an ideal construct, built outside time and space, but complete in the mind of the author. It is realized in time and space, by pen, ink, and paper, or by wire, silicon, and ferrite. …the incompleteness and inconsistencies of our ideas become clear only in implementation”

— Frederick P. Brooks, The Mythical Man-Month, p15

I don’t know about you, but most of my ideas are not at all complete when their still in my mind. I found this out the hard way many times when trying to code my ideas, and then found it much, much easier when poking those holes while it was still on my notebook.

Often, those holes weren’t poked until a few days later.
